\begin{abstract}
Speech Large Language Models (Speech LLMs) are moving from benchmarks
into voice assistants and full-duplex systems, but existing surveys
describe how they are built, not whether they can be trusted in
deployment. We audit the field through a lifecycle lens spanning
training, evaluation, deployment, and responsible design. Our corpus
comprises 18 Speech LLM benchmarks published in 2024--2026 (3
peer-reviewed, 15 arXiv preprints), coded across 17 capability
dimensions into an 18$\times$17 coverage matrix. Four findings anchor
the analysis. First, benchmark coverage is skewed: ASR, spoken question
answering, and emotion each appear in 12/18, while safety appears in
4/18, tool use in 2/18, latency in 1/18, and privacy in 0/18. Second, a
scope--lens taxonomy reveals an empty Use-case$\times$Capability cell,
so practitioners cannot select a Speech LLM for a realistic job while
preserving a specific capability from published scores. Third, a
reverse audit shows six interaction-first systems (Moshi, GPT-4o-Audio,
MiniCPM-o~4.5, Voila, OmniFlatten, Gemini~Live) each appear in at most
one benchmark, exposing a mismatch between deployed capability and
public record. Fourth, five documented cross-stage couplings show
training, evaluation, deployment, and safety are not independent. From
these findings we propose five benchmark design principles, Deployment
and Evaluation Card templates, and a governance framework covering
audio jailbreaks, voice cloning, speaker re-identification, and
provenance under transformation.
\end{abstract}

\begin{highlights}
\item Audit of 18 Speech LLM benchmarks (2024--2026) shows privacy 0/18, latency 1/18, safety 4/18.
\item Two-axis taxonomy exposes an empty Use-case$\times$Capability cell.
\item Six interaction-first systems appear in $\leq$1 benchmark each, a temporal record gap.
\item Five documented training--evaluation--deployment--safety couplings drive the recommendations.
\item Deployment and Evaluation Card templates enable joint reporting of accuracy, latency, and safety.
\end{highlights}
