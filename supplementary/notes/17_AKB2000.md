## 1. Motivation (≤ 5 lines)

LLMs serve as knowledge backbones for large audio language models (LALMs), yet it remains unclear how much auditory knowledge they encode through text-only pre-training and how this affects downstream audio performance. Most LALM studies select a single LLM without justifying the choice based on the backbone's auditory knowledge. This work systematically investigates whether different LLM families manifest different levels of auditory understanding and whether text-only performance predicts multimodal performance after fine-tuning.

## 4. Evaluation protocol

- **Metric type**: Accuracy (%)
- **Scoring details**: Multiple-choice QA with 4 options per question, uniform distribution ensuring 25% random baseline. GPT-4o employed as judge to verify responses match ground-truth for cascade/audio-grounded settings. Pearson correlation computed across metrics.
- **Models evaluated in the paper**: 12 open-weight LLMs (Qwen, Llama, Phi, OLMo families; 4B-14B scale); 5 proprietary models (GPT-5, GPT-4o, Gemini-2.5-Pro, Gemini-2.0-Flash, Claude-Sonnet-4.5)
- **Reproducibility**: Code, benchmarks, and model checkpoints will be released. DeSTA framework used for fine-tuning with identical training recipe. Whisper-large-v3 audio encoder + 6-layer Q-Former connector, frozen LLM params during training. vLLM used for serving open-weight models with default generation config.

## 5. Reported limitations

- L1: Proprietary LLMs were involved in AKB-2000 curation, so their near-saturated performance (94–96%) represents upper bound rather than unbiased comparison.
- L2: LLMs systematically fail on phonological tasks (10–15 percentage point lag across all categories) due to fundamental limitation of text-only pre-training—models cannot access acoustic realization of language despite semantic token correlations.
- L3: Cascade evaluation bottlenecked by captioner quality; even strongest captioner (Gemini-2.5-Pro) + top LLM plateaus at ~70%, and audio encoder appears to be primary bottleneck in end-to-end LALMs rather than LLM backbone.

## 8. One-paragraph summary (≤ 120 words, narrative form)

This paper investigates how auditory knowledge encoded in LLM backbones shapes the performance of large audio language models through three complementary evaluations: direct probing on AKB-2000 (a new 2,000-question benchmark covering sound, music, paralinguistics, phonetics, and audio quality), cascade evaluation where audio is captioned then passed to text-only LLMs, and audio-grounded evaluation via end-to-end fine-tuning. Evaluating 12 open-weight and 5 proprietary LLMs, the authors show that auditory knowledge varies substantially across families (Qwen consistently outperforms Llama by >10 points), text-only performance strongly correlates with audio-grounded results (r=0.71–0.82), and simple cascade pipelines match or exceed several state-of-the-art LALMs, suggesting audio encoders are the primary bottleneck rather than LLM backbones.
